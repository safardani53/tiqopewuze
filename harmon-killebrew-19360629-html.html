Harmon Killebrew (Baseball Player) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Harmon Clayton Killebrew Jr., nicknamed 'The Killer' and 'Hammerin' Harmon', was an American professional baseball first baseman, third baseman, and left fielder. He was a prolific power hitter who spent most of his 22-year career in Major League Baseball with the Minnesota Twins. At the time of his retirement Killebrew had the fourth-most home runs in major league history. He was second only to Babe Ruth in American League home runs, and was the AL career leader in home runs by a right-handed batter. Killebrew was inducted into the National Baseball Hall of Fame in 1984. Killebrew was 5-foot-11-inch tall and 213 pounds. His compact swing generated tremendous power and made him one of the most feared power hitters of the 1960s when he hit at least 40 home runs in a season eight times. In total Killebrew led the league six times in home runs and three times in RBIs, and was named to thirteen All-Star teams.

Baseball Player Harmon Killebrew was born in Payette, Idaho, United States on June 29, 1936. He dies at the age of 74, on May 17, 2011.

He was the American League MVP in 1969 and a 13-time All-Star who hit 573 home runs during his career, garnering him the moniker"Hammerin' Harmon". He played for the Minnesota Twins for all but one of his 22 MLB seasons, even while the franchise was known as the Senators and was based in Washington.
