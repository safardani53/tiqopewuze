Woman, 72, dies after 'machete-wielding son-in-law set her on fire then torched himself' at her 80

A PENSIONER who was set alight by her machete-wielding former son-in-law in a horrific cul-de-sac attack has died from her injuries.

Kieren Lynch, 50, set upon Jennifer Cronin, 72,  in the garden of her Â£800,000 home next to a golf course.

Shocked witnesses described how Lynch waved a machete and poured petrol over himself and Mrs Cronin before lighting the fuel.

The pair both suffered horrific burns and were airlifted to hospital in a critical condition - with Mrs Cronin hanging on for 17 days.

Lynch was arrested on suspicion of attempted murder after the brutal attack on March 13 but died from his injuries soon after.

Mrs Cronin lived alone at the property in Benfleet, Essex, and the motive for the attack remains unclear.

A spokesman for Essex Police said: "A woman has died following a disturbance in Benfleet. The 72-year-old suffered serious burns during the incident within the garden of a property in Boyce Green, Benfleet, on Tuesday, March 13.

"She was taken to hospital in a critical condition but sadly died on Friday, March 30.

"Kieren Lynch, 50, from Canvey, was arrested on suspicion of attempted murder in connection with the disturbance.

"He had also suffered burns and died in hospital on March 13."

Mrs Cronin's quiet cul-de-sac was flooded with police, fire engines and ambulances in the wake of the brutality.

One witness said: "A man walked up to a house armed with a machete and he looked like he wanted to attack someone.

"He found a woman in the house and he set her on fire. He then went into the back garden of the house and set himself on fire.

"Then a woman came running down the street screaming for help saying someone had set her mother-in-law on fire. It was an absolutely horrific incident - totally shocking."

A stunned friend of Mrs Cronin said "all hell broke loose" after the attack which took place at around 11am.

Police taped off the detached house where the violence unfolded, with a BMW and a Fiat with personalised plates sealed behind the cordon.

A neighbour said: "We are talking about a widow in her early 70's but she wasn't the one who instigated anything.

"The way the police are going there's been a fatality. She knew the person who did it and his wife turned up and the granddaughter's car is there too.

"The widow lives there on her own, she lost her husband about 18 months ago.

"I don't know the guy who did it, but it is family.

"I just come out having a shower and all hell broke loose. "This is a quiet street, it so quiet people don't move.

"It is generally pensioners down here. It is just so sleepy."

Another neighbour said: "She's a lovely lady. We're just all in shock at the moment.

"Hopefully police can work out what the hell has gone on and why."

"Alternatively, contact the Crimestoppers charity 100% anonymously on 0800 555 111 or use the anonymous online form at Crimestoppers-uk.org"
