Apollo Crews | Early Life and Family

Apollo Crews was born in Sacramento, California, United States of America. His birth name is Sesugh Uhaa. Unfortunately, the names of his parents are not available.

He is said to have a sister. His sister is in the United States Army, stationed in San Antonio, Texas.

He is of Nigerian descent. His father originally hails from Benue State in the mid-belt region of Nigeria. His mother is from Uganda. They separated after a few years of marriage.

Sesugh Uhaa lived with his father initially in Uganda. His mother raised his sister in Atlanta, Georgia. The separated family used to fly over to the other part very often for a reunion.

Apollo Crews | Professional Wrestling Career

Uhaa managed to get a job to pay for his professional wrestling training right after finishing college.

He trained under Curtis “Mr.” Hughes at his World Wrestling Alliance 4 (WWA4) promotion training school in Atlanta. He was 21 then.

Uhaa made his wrestling debut under the ring name Uhaa Nation on August 17, 2009. He then passed over a year working mainly for small promotions on the Georgian independent circuit.

At the same time, he managed to make trips to Houston, Texas-based Pro Wrestling Alliance (PWA), and Phenix City, Alabama-based Great Championship Wrestling (GCW). In addition, he worked with fellow WWA4 trainee AR Fox very often.

Dragon Gate and affiliates

Early 2011

Uhaa was a participant at a tryout seminar held by the Dragon Gate USA promotion on September 9, 2011.  He then immediately signed for appearances for Dragon Gate USA. Moreover, he signed for its parent promotion, Dragon Gate.

Similarly, he was associated with its close affiliates, Evolve and Full Impact Pro (FIP).

He debuted for the Dragon Gate USA later that same day. In fact, he succeeded in defeating Aaron Draven in a squash match.

He then debuted in pay-per-view debut at Untouchable 2011 the following day. He accepted Brodie Lee’s open challenge and ended up dominating him. Lee then left the ringside area.

Uhaa then defeated Facade, Flip Kendrick, and Sugar Dunkerton in a four-way match the following day. It was at the Way of the Ronin 2011 pay-per-view.

WWE

Uhaa participated in a WWE tryout camp in October 2014. He was then offered a developmental contract.

It was reported that Uhaa had come to terms on a contract with WWE since the last of 2014.

2015

Uhaa Nation then reported to the WWE Performance Center on April 6, 2015.

WWE announced Uhaa’s participation in a new NXT recruits class in a press release on April 13.

Uhaa was seen on the television for the first time in association with WWE on the May 6 episode of NXT. He was seen signing an NXT contract in a segment with William Regal.

He got associated with the NXT house shows in May 2015. His ring name, the Uhaa Nation, was retained.

He was then introduced with a new ring name, Apollo Crews, on August 5. Crews debuted on the televised NXT on August 22 at NXT TakeOver: Brooklyn.

He debuted at the August 13 tapings. It was scheduled to air after NXT TakeOver: Brooklyn, defeating Martin Stone.

Brooklyn, Crews defeated Tye Dillinger in his official debut game at NXT TakeOver.

Crews had a win in a battle royal to become the #1 contender in the NXT Championship at the October 8 tapings of NXT. The title was given to him over Finn Bálor on October 22.

However, Finn had won the match. But he could not win the title owing to some sort of disqualification.

Baron Corbin then attacked Apollo. Unfortunately, Corbin had bad blood against Crews as he eliminated him from the #1 contender’s battle royal.

The rivalry between Crews and Corbin turned into an official feud. It was held at NXT TakeOver: London, which Corbin won.

2016

He then had a small feud against Elias Samson. It started after he saved Johnny Gargano from Elias’ assault on March 23, 2016, NXT.

The rivals were scheduled to have a match on the April 6 NXT. Crews became the winner.

Main roster (2016–present)

Crews debuted on the main roster on April 4, 2016. It was on Raw, defeating Tyler Breeze. He then got into a feud with the Social Outcasts. He succeeded in defeating each stable member, culminating in a match on the April 18 Raw.

Mid 2016

The SmackDown brand drafted him in the 2016 WWE draft on July 19. Crews won a battle royal on July 16. He earned a spot in a six-pack challenge, determining the #1 contender to Dean Ambrose’s WWE World Championship at SummerSlam.

Dolph Ziggler then defeated him in the six-pack challenge. It also had AJ Styles, Baron Corbin, Bray Wyatt, and John Cena.

He had a win over Corbin and Kalisto the following week. Consequently, he became the #1 contender to the WWE Intercontinental Championship.

He got his title shot at SummerSlam on August 21. However, The Mix defeated him.

Crews then had a win over The Miz in a non-title match at the 2016 Tribute to the Troops. It led to a title match on the December 20 SmackDown. Unfortunately, the Miz retained the title.
