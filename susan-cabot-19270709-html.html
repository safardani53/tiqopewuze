Susan Cabot is an American film, theater and television actress. She became famous for her roles in many Western films, including Tomahawk, The Duel at Silver Creek and Gunsmoke. After breaking his contract with Universal Pictures in the mid-1950s, Cabot returned to perform in New York theaters. She then returned to Hollywood at the end of the decade and appeared in a series of films directed by Roger Corman, such as The School Girl, War of the Satellites and Machine-Gun Kelly. She appeared in her last film in Corman's horror film, The Wasp Woman. Cabot spent the next two decades largely in isolation, although she appeared in off-Broadway theater in the early 1960s, and appeared on television in 1970 in the Bracken's World series. In the 1980s, Cabot suffered from serious mental illnesses, including depression, suicidal thoughts and irrational phobias.
