Inside Harry Styles' secret training regime to keep him six-pack ready during his Love on Tour shows

HERE’S the proof that Harry Styles gives it his all on and off stage.

The As It Was hitmaker, who wowed the crowds at his Love On Tour gigs at Wembley Stadium, was spotted working up a sweat in the scorching heat.

Harry tied his floppy locks into bun and showed off his tattoos as he went topless for a workout run, under the watchful eye of his personal trainer Brad Gould.

Ahead of his much-anticipated London shows — which finished tonight — Harry made sure to take some time out and splashed £10,000 on a luxury stay at celebrity favourite Soho Farmhouse in Oxfordshire.

A source told me: “Harry snuck off for a long weekend with pals to fully recharge the batteries before heading to London.

“Harry loves nothing more than training with his mates in the great outdoors and tucking into fresh organic food.

“He wants to be on top of his game for the tour and investing in his wellbeing has become a huge priority.”

But it’s personal trainer Brad who has been credited for getting Harry into shape.

The strength and conditioning coach, from Bournemouth, even accompanied the Brit Award-winner on the Australian leg of the tour to keep him on track.

Harry is heading to Cardiff this week for two more shows before hitting the Continent for a series of gigs, finishing in Italy next month.

But while the singer channels rock ‘n’ roll icons such as Mick Jagger on stage, he shuns the boozy lifestyle.

Instead, the hitmaker practises Pilates when he is not on stage and told fashion Bible Vogue: “I’ve got very tight hamstrings.”

The former One Direction star has also been spotted taking a dip in Hampstead Heath ponds — near his London pad — and also revealed that he meditates twice a day to keep his mental health in check.

He said: “It has changed my life. It’s helped me just be more present.

“I feel like I’m able to enjoy the things that are happening right in front of me, even if it’s food or it’s coffee or it’s being with a friend — or a swim in a really cold pond!

“Meditation just brings a stillness that has been really beneficial, I think, for my mental health.”

If his recent gigs are anything to go by, his fellow popstars should take a leaf out of his book.

HOWARD: I HAVE A LOT TO LEARN

HOWARD DONALD has been axed from a Pride event after fans discovered he had liked a series of homophobic and transphobic tweets.

The Take That star, who was due to headline at Groovebox in Nottingham next month, deleted his Twitter account and apologised.

He posted on Instagram: “I have made a huge error in my judgment liking social media posts that are derogatory towards the LGBTQIA+ community.

“I am really disappointed in myself and I am sorry for any hurt that I have caused by my uneducated actions. I clearly have a lot to learn and it’s a priority for me that I do this.”

Drag Race’s Danny Beard, who will perform at the festival, was outraged by the tweets that the singer liked.

One called for Disney to be “defunded” for holding a Pride event.

Prior to the social media storm, Donald had been promoting Take That movie musical Greatest Days, appearing at its premiere in London on Thursday.

Gay rights activist Peter Tatchell told me: “Pride events should not be hosting artists who promote prejudice.

“I am sure Howard was booked without the organisers knowing about the intolerance that he has stoked.”

A spokesman for the festival said: “Howard Donald will no longer be playing at our event.

“We would like to offer our thanks to the public for alerting us to the situation.”
