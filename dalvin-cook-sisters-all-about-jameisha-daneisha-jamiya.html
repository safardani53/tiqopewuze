All About Jameisha, Daneisha And Jamiya

Dalvin Cook’s sisters, Daneisha, Jameisha, and Jamaiya Cook, reside in Miami, Florida. All three are younger than Dalvin and share a close bond with him.

Though they might not see each other every day or even live in the same state, the Cook siblings are a family that treasures being together.

After their father’s demise in 2020, Dalvin Cook further stepped up for his younger siblings.

In his interview, Cook said if he breaks down, then his siblings, who are already torn, will become weak.

Along with three younger sisters, Dalvin has an older brother in DeAndre, a half-brother in Demarcus, and a younger brother in James Cook.

Cook has been recently released by the Minnesota Vikings, bringing an end to his six long years with them.

There is a high possibility that Dalvin will link up with Tyreek Hill and Tua Tagovailoa at Miami, according to early reports. 

Dalvin Cook And His Three Sisters

The former Vikings player, Dalvin Cook, has three younger sisters. The three sisters, Daneshia, Jameisha, and Jamiya Cook, are all residents of Miami, Florida.

The second oldest of five children between James Cook and Varondria White, Dalvin shares a tight bond with his sisters.

The oldest of the three sisters, Daneisha Cook, was born on October 9, 1996, and is a year younger than Dalvin. She is a graduate of American Senior High School and remains private about her life. 

Daneisha has previously featured on her mom, Varondria’s Instagram handle, with Varondria sharing an appreciation post for her on her birthday last year. 

Jameisha And Jamiya Are Graduates Of Miami Central Senior High School

Jameisha and Jamiya are the two youngsters of the Cook household. The second youngest of five, Jameisha was born on July 19, 2001, and is six years younger than Dalvin. 

She graduated from Miami Center Senior High School in 2019, and two years later, her youngest sister, Jamaiya, followed her lead.

Jamaiya was born on September 13, 2002, and previously worked at Northside Panthers Optimist Club.

She is available on Instagram with the username @priiincess_.j and has previously shared Instagram stories in the memories of her late father.

Dalvin Cook Parents: James And Varondria White

Dalvin Cook’s parents, James and Varondria White, divorced when Dalvin was quite young. 

Though the running back always shared a tight bond with his parents, Dalvin was much closer to his dad, James.

Dalvin Shared A Strong Bond With His Dad

Dalvin started living with his father and grandmother, popularly known as Miss Betty, in her community at 13. With Betty, James would attend all of Dalvin’s games at Florida State. 

He owned a mobile car wash business and loved cooking Chinese chicken fried rice for his son. The father-son duo would go fishing together though neither was too good at it.

It was a dream come true for James when he saw his son become an NFL player in 2017. The father of six passed away on December 29, 2020, due to complications from diabetes. 

In his interview with ESPN, Dalvin said he saw it unfold during a Facetime call with his grandmom. Only the night before his father’s demise, Cook had received a call from James late at night.

The running back did wonder why his dad had called him late at night, knowing he would already be asleep. While on the call with his grandmom, Cook saw his father take his last breath. 

He had decided to dedicate the 2021 season to his late father, but he missed out on the majority of the games because of his injuries. 

His Mom Varondria Is His Biggest Supporter 

As he waited patiently for his name to be called out in the 2017 NFL Draft, Dalvin was closely seated with his mom, Varondria. 

He still has the photo of his mom hugging him as his name got called out on his Instagram. Despite their divorce, Varondria and James continued to raise their kids amicably.

Varondria works as a home health aide and studies at ATI Health College. She tied the knot for the second time on June 29, 2019, with Terence White. 

Her Instagram handle is dedicated to her six kids, for whom she continues sharing appreciation posts on their achievements and birthdays.
