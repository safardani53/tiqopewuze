Roberto Carlos (Soccer Player) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Roberto Carlos da Silva Rocha, commonly known as Roberto Carlos, is a Brazilian former professional footballer who now works as a football ambassador. He started his career in Brazil as a forward but spent most of his career as a left back and has been described as the "most offensive-minded left-back in the history of the game". Carlos is also widely considered one of the best left backs in history, and was also known as a free kick specialist throughout his career—his bending shots have measured at over 105 miles per hour. In 1997, he was runner-up in the FIFA World Player of the Year. He was chosen on the FIFA World Cup Dream Team, and in 2004 was named by Pelé in the FIFA 100 list of the world's greatest living players. At club level, he joined Real Madrid in 1996 where he spent 11 highly successful seasons, playing 584 matches in all competitions and scoring 71 goals. At Real, he won four La Liga titles and the UEFA Champions League three times.

Soccer Player Roberto Carlos was born on April 10, 1973 in Sao Paulo, Brazil (He's 50 years old now).

In 2002, he was a key member of the Brazilian national team that helped them win the World Cup. Between 1996 and 2007, he played in 370 appearances for Team Real Madrid and was crowned UEFA Defender of the Year in 2002 and 2003.
