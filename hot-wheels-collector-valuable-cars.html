I'm a Hot Wheels collector - check your home for these five models worth up to $5,000 including one

HOT Wheels cars are more than just toys, they can also be valuable collectibles.

A TikToker recently shared a video of some of the most valuable mini-vehicles in his collection.

Christian (@hotwheelhistory) regularly shares fun facts about the vintage toys in his collection.

In the video, he showed some valuable but common Hot Wheels that could be hiding in your collection.

1. BROWN CUSTOM CAMARO

This car is so common that one person commented: "[I] think everyone had the brown Camaro."

Despite the mass production of different Camaros by Hot Wheels, the 1968 version with a white interior is special.

read more on collectors

It is one of the rarest and some people claim it was only used for display purposes.

Collectors have paid up to $2,000 to have one.

2. WHITE CUSTOM CAMARO

Similar to the brown one, this 1968 white Camaro was reportedly a prototype for designers to use, according to Car and Driver.

They were never meant to be sold, but some were mistakenly packaged and sent to stores.

Most read in Money

They have been sold for as much as $3,000.

3. GREEN OPEN FIRE

The 1972 Open Fire came in five different colors, and the most sought-after one is green.

It was designed to look like a stretched AMC Gremlin, a classic car.

4. RED FERRARI 312P

This 1970 Ferrari is almost exclusively found with a black interior.

The rare white interior is highly sought after by collectors.

It has sold for $5,000 before.

5. PURPLE BYE FOCAL

The Spectraflame purple car had an iridescent hue that was a hit with fans.

Unfortunately, they're hard to come by as they're subject to "crumbling," a condition in which the toy develops cracks and falls apart over time.

Due to its rarity, it sells for around $6,000.
