Savage thugs batter taxi driver dad after he asked them to stop setting fire to his seats

A TAXI driver's children have been left horrified after their dad was left with bloody gashes to his face from a brutal attack at the hands of two passengers.

Sheikh Arbaz was horrifically battered after he asked two customers in his cab to stop burning the back seats with a lighter while taking them home  in the early hours of last Saturday morning.

But the passengers demanded money before punching the 43-year-old in face - with the attack only coming to an end after residents, who heard the taxi driver's screams, rushed out to help.

The father-of-four managed to make it home with Shaz Saleem, chairman of the Dudley Private Hire & Taxi Association, saying: "It’s heartbreaking. When he got home, his two daughters aged three and five were absolutely shocked to see his face and they were like ‘Daddy, what happened?’

"He told them that he fell over because it’s not nice to be attacked at work. How do you face your family after that has happened?"

Mr Arbaz, who drives for ABM Taxis, picked the pair up from his firm’s base near Stourbridge town centre and they originally asked to be taken to the Village Hotel in Dudley, West Midlands on about 4.30am on February 4.

But the pair then changed their minds, asking to go to Hallchurch Road, Dudley - where they started to burn the taxi's seats with a lighter.

The taxi driver is then said to have challenged the men, who instead got out of the car and demanded money.

Mr Saleem said: "He offered to give them £40 to £50 and they started to punch him.

"He doesn’t remember much after that.

"There was screaming and noise and the neighbours came out and they panicked and ran off.”

His two daughters aged three and five were absolutely shocked to see his face and they were like ‘Daddy, what happened?’ How do you face your family after that has happened?”

Mr Arbaz was taken to the nearby Russell’s Hall hospital where he received stitches to his face.

He is now recovering at home and is off the road.

Mr Saleem said: "We are angry with the whole situation. We do not believe that any professional body should be working in an unsafe environment."

West Midlands police said in a statement: "Police are investigating after a taxi driver was attacked by two men who he had taken from his taxi base in Stourbridge to Hallchurch Road in Holly Hall, Dudley in the early hours of last Saturday morning.

"Officers are examining CCTV in the area and are asking anyone with information to call police on 101 quoting reference no. 20DY/24611R/17."
