Born Mary Elizabeth Truss in Oxford, England, she has gone by "Elizabeth" since an early age. Her family moved to Scotland when she was four. She also spent a year in Canada. She graduated from Merton College, Oxford in 1996, and joined the Conservative party that year. She worked for Shell and Cable & Wireless before being elected to House of Commons in 2010.

After then-PM Politician Boris Johnson appointed Truss Foreign Secretary in September 2021, a review of the government's official account on the photo-sharing platform Flickr revealed that since the day she'd taken office, more than 700 pictures had been uploaded featuring an image of herself--an average of more than four-and-a-half a day, or about one photograph for every five hours on the job.

Her father is an emeritus professor of pure mathematics at the University of Leeds. Her mother was a nurse, teacher, and member of the Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament. They were both slightly left of center. When she ran for Parliament as a Conservative, her mother campaigned for her but her father declined. In 2000, she married Hugh O'Leary, an accountant whom she first met at the 1997 Conservative Party Conference. They have two daughters.
