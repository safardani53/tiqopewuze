Furious gran fined 90 for overstaying in M&amp;S car park when grandson, 3, was violently sick in stor

A GRAN today branded a parking company “disgusting” after she was slapped with a fine for overstaying at a retail park when her grandson was taken violently ill.

Anita Wilson, 57, was found to be 28 minutes over the time limit during a visit to an M&S Food Hall in the Solent Retail Park in Havant, Hants.

She was delayed after three-year-old Alfie McHugh vomited three times - requiring first aid treatment from shop staff.

Now, after a failed appeal against the penalty issued by Euro Car Parks in March - she has been ordered to pay £90.

The Tesco worker, from nearby Waterlooville, said: "I am absolutely disgusted that I am expected to pay this fine.

"There was no way I could have known my grandson was going to be sick like that just as I was returning to the car.

"Obviously, I could not have just left him in the store while I went outside to move my car knowing how unwell he was – that’s ridiculous.

"He was asleep in his pushchair, he woke up and was very sick, three times.

"I thought I was going to be sick myself."

A spokesperson for M&S said: "We are supporting Mrs Wilson and working with Euro Car Parks to come to an agreed resolution."

Anita’s husband, 55 year old Nigel, said: "I can understand the fine standing if it was a customer who had deliberately stayed to shop.

"But I cannot see how the company can stand by its decision to charge in this unusual circumstance. It just seems like a money-making exercise."

Euro Car Parks have been approached for comment.
