Margaret Magdalen Heinz Karcher is an American fast food pioneer who co-founded burger chain Carl's Jr. with her husband Carl Kärcher, now owned by parent company CKE Restaurants. Kärcher and her husband started their first business, a sausage stand, on July 17, 1941, in Los Angeles, California, when they borrowed $ 311 to buy their Plymouth car and add $ 15 to Margaret's wallet. Initially the stall sold Mexican sausages and tamales. On January 16, 1945, they opened their first restaurant, Carl's Drive-In Bar Grill in Anaheim, California. They also opened Carl's Jr. Their restaurant grew rapidly and they opened a number of other restaurants, numbering 100 in 1974 and over 300 in 1981 .
