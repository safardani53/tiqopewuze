Manu Vunipola set for his Saracens debut to join cousins Billy and Mako in the big leagues

WATCH OUT rugby. . . here comes ANOTHER Vunipola!

As if England's rivals didn't fear them enough with world-class forwards Mako and Billy causing havoc - there's now another family member related to the terrorising Tongans on the scene.

Younger cousin Manu is set for his Saracens debut off the bench today in their top-two Premiership clash with Exeter Chiefs.

The England under-20s fly-half is the son of Elisi, brother of Mako and Billy's dad Feʻao, and himself an ex-Super Rugby star with the Brumbies in Australia.

Manu's dad was also Tonga's most-capped player until 2005 and it is his playmaker father, not the ferocious forwards Mako and Billy, who he cites as his biggest inspiration.

Manu said: “He helps me a lot with understanding the game and on improving my game whenever I’m at home.

"He will tell me what improvements I can make whenever he watches any of my matches.”

Meanwhile, England boss Eddie Jones has been told that his scrum-half headache has been solved.

Saracens chief Mark McCall reckons the England boss would be crazy not to pick his star No9 Ben Spencer who has been flying for the Londoners as they chase a historic double.

The 26-year-old, capped just three times, will start for his side in the European Cup final against Leinster a week today and has been one of the outstanding scrum-halves in Europe and the Premiership this season.

Ben Spencer's self-belief has gone through the roof. All of a sudden you feel a little bit different about yourself. Sometimes you just need to feel a million dollars - and he does now.

Mark McCall

So with Jones still undecided on his back-up to Leicester Tigers ace Ben Youngs, McCall reckons the case is now closed with

Harlequins back Danny Care out of favour and Wasps pocket-rocket Dan Robson struggling with blood clots in his legs.

Asked if Spencer should start packing his bags for the World Cup, McCall said: "I would be surprised if he's not in Japan.

"His self-belief has gone through the roof. All of a sudden you feel a little bit different about yourself.

"He has always been skilful and a good enough passer, quick enough and a good kicker.

"Sometimes you just need to feel a million dollars - and he does now. Had he been exposed to international rugby when he was 22 I'm not sure how would have been ready.

"This is exactly the perfect time for him, I do admire his patience, but there has always been a plan and he knew when his starts and benches were coming.

"His battle has been to get selected for European Cup matches and that is what is happening at the moment."

Spencer has been wrapped up in cotton wool for today's game against Exeter Chiefs with both sides having booked a home play-off place.

The likely dress rehearsal for the Premiership final on June 1 is also a chance for McCall to rest a whole string of his key men, with full-back Alex Goode also getting the chance to put his feet up.

Goode is a front-runner for the Champions Cup player-of-the-season award, but is shockingly still ignored at Test level by Jones.

And McCall said: "We want all of our players to get what they deserve and he has been brilliant for our club for so long. Sometimes it is hard to fathom.

"That's not in our control, but what is in our control is how well Alex is playing and most people think he's playing pretty well.
