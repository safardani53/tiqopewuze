Family Feud fans shocked by 'nasty' insult to Taylor Swift as survey says she's not a 'good singer'

FAMILY Feud has shocked viewers after indirectly insulting Taylor Swift during a recent episode.

Fans have been sharing their thoughts after the popular game show featured Taylor, 33, in one of its prompts.

The singer - who was just named Time Magazine's Person of the Year - has been spiking in popularity with the success of her Eras Tour and new romance with NFL star Travis Kelce, 34.

Longtime Family Feud host Steve Harvey, 66, brought two contestants to the stand as he read aloud: "Name a reason it's good to be Taylor Swift."

The first answer given was for her "long beautiful blonde hair," which fell under the second spot on the board.

With only four spots, the other contestant noted Taylor's high net worth, which landed the number one spot.

After choosing to play, rather than pass, the next contestant on her team answered: "She's a great singer."

However, the game's large red "X" buzzed on the screen, as it didn't make the list of the most-given answers in the survey.

The third answer on the board was that she is "famous/popular," while the fourth was that she is "young."

Last week, Taylor was also the hot topic of another popular game show, where she was seemingly snubbed once more.

SNUBBED AGAIN?

The contestants on the December 6 episode of Celebrity Jeopardy seemed to save one category for last, leaving viewers curious as to why.

Contestants Kyra Sedgwick, Mo Rocca, and Amanda Seales faced off in the last quarterfinal game of the season, hosted by Ken Jennings, 49.

During the Double Jeopardy! round, there was a category titled: "Also A Taylor Swift Song."

Although the Shake It Off singer's fans would have jumped at the opportunity to choose that category, the celebrities did not.

It was the last category left on the board at the end of the round, and it was virtually untouched.

Fans of the show on X couldn't fathom why Taylor's category was ignored.

“They're avoiding the Taylor Swift category like the plague. Way to disrespect the newly minted Time Person of the Year," one tweeted.
