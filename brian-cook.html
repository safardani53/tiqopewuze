Brian Cook | Early Career

Cook attended Lincoln Community High School and played for the school basketball team, Railsplitters. He led the team to the 1999 Illinois High School Association Class AA state boys basketball championship quarterfinals.

As a result, he was honored a McDonald’s All-American in 1999 and was chosen to the 1998 State Farm Holiday Classic all-tournament team.

Furthermore, he was also awarded the title of 1999 Illinois Mr. Basketball. Cook was inducted into the Hall of Fame of the Illinois Basketball Coaches Association in 2016. 

After a stellar high school career, Cook played for the college basketball team at the University of Illinois.

Also, Cook was a skilled scorer who could easily hit three-point shots. Thus, he was named co-Big Ten Freshman of the Year.

Furthermore, he was awarded multiple titles and honors, including Big Ten Conference’s Most Valuable Player, Big Ten Player of the Year, and first-team All-Big Ten.

Additionally, Cook graduated as the third all-time highest scorer at Illinois, with 1,748 total points and an average of 13.2 points per game.

In 2008, Cook’s jersey was one of the thirty-three honored jerseys at the State Farm Center. He was honored for being the most decorated basketball player in the history of the University of Illinois.

Cook was mainly deployed as a three-point specialist. The athlete saw an increase in playing time, and people started to acknowledge his skills.
