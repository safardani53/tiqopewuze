RHOA's Drew Sidora claims husband 'disappeared for three days' then spied on her and kids in 'roller

REAL Housewives Of Atlanta new star Drew Sidora claimed her husband, Ralph Pittman, "disappeared for three days."

She further claimed he then spied on her and their kids in their "roller coaster" marriage.

During last night's episode of RHOA, fans got to meet the new housewife, Drew, who was originally introduced to the group as Eva Marcille's friend last season and is now close friends with Cynthia Bailey.

They also got a glimpse into her six-year marriage to the IT entrepreneur as she described their relationship to producers: "It can be quite acrobatic, quite unbalanced.

"We're best friends, we get along when we love, but when it's low, it's super low. And we're arguing about things in year six that we argued about like, year one."

However, Ralph revealed to Mike Hill as the two spent some time together during Cynthia's get-together with the ladies that he's had to "just get out and just go" following the Covid-19 lockdown and Drew's mother crashing at their place during the pandemic.

At the same party, Drew revealed to the ladies that she and her husband have considered divorce multiple times.

She told them: "I've divorced my husband like, every other day. My husband will say we're getting a divorce.

"Like we've said, 'Okay, we're going to get our own places, we're going to get lawyers.' I'm telling you for real, last week he left and didn't tell me where he was."

She continued: "He was gone for like, three days. My husband is a runner, so he likes to go and he doesn't want to answer anybody."

The actress then explained in a confessional that she and Ralph "got into a huge argument," and that he "was at a breaking point for sure."

She continued: "So he went to his office, you know, to cool off, and later that night when I went to check on him, he was gone. And I didn't see him for three days."

Things reached an all-time high between Drew and Ralph as they continued to celebrate their anniversary with a chef-cooked meal at home.

"I feel like that's just not a part of the marriage," Drew told him when he asked what she thought wasn't working between them.

"You don't ever put that ingredient, like, in the marriage. â€¦ You feel comfortable just leaving and not telling me that you're leaving. That's one major issue. I'd like to see that change.

"When you go for a trip, do you feel like it's important to tell your wife that you're leaving, A?" she asked.

"And B, that you're going for days and where you were? Because you left the other day and I still don't know where you were."

The new housewife continued: "You left and I didn't know where you were. You didn't take my phone calls, you didn't talk to me, you didn't text me back unless it was about the house or about business."

Ralph hit back, saying: "Go check your text messages and not one single text message went without being returned in one minute."

However, he refused to tell her where he had been exactly, aside from saying he was at the beach, as it would have not been beneficial to their relationship.

He then admitted that he had left Georgia, which shocked Drew even more.

She told him: "If there's an emergency, or anything that happens, I should know where my husband is. We all thought you were downstairs. I got you dinner, I'm banging on the door. How do I not know my husband left the house?"

Finally, Ralph broke and told her he had been in Tampa, Florida.

But Drew's questions weren't done when she dropped a major bomb, asking him: "You have a camera in the house that watches us? Were you watching us when you were gone?"

Ralph admitted that he had "cameras everywhere" but that he hadn't seen the footage from the days he had been gone.

She told him: "You were watching us in the family room. Me and my mom and the kids. I didn't know there was a camera there, and I saw the camera and you were watching us. So now you're lying to me, that's what we're doing? That's crazy."

The mother of three then admitted that Ralph has "always been 'big brother.'

"He's into this technology. I found a picture in his phone where I was sleeping, my mom was sitting on the couch and my kids were in the background. It's just too much. Like, that's disrespectful. It's an invasion of privacy. And I am so hurt."

"So you knew where I was, but I didn't know where you were," said Drew. "You see how lopsided that is?"
