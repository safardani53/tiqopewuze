Jules Verne (Novelist) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Jules Gabriel Verne is a French novelist, poet and playwright. His collaboration with publisher Pierre-Jules Hetzel led to the creation of extra-story tours, a series of successful adventure novels including Journey to the Center of Earth, Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea and Around the World in Eighty Days. Verne is considered an important author in France and most of Europe, where he had a great influence on pioneer literature and surrealism. His reputation was noticeably different in the English-speaking regions, where he was often considered a writer of novels or children's books, largely due to the reduced and edited translations for which his novels are often printed. Since the 1980s, his literary reputation has continued to improve. Verne is the second most translated author in the world since 1979, standing between Agatha Christie and William Shakespeare.

Beginning at the age of six, he attended a French boarding school and was strongly inspired by his teacher's tales of her navy captain husband, who had gone missing at sea. Verne began writing long works in his adolescence, and his father sent him to Paris to study law.

For works such as Journey to the Center of the Earth and 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea, Verne has been labeled the"Father of Science Fiction".

He, his brother, and three sisters were raised by attorney Pierre Verne and Sophie Allote de la Fuyeand. His son, Novelist Michel Verne, was born in late summer 1861 to him and his wife, Honorine de Viane Morel.
